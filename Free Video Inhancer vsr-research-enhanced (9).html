01 — Abstract

Video Super-Resolution (VSR) presents a fundamental challenge in computer vision — reconstructing high-resolution frames from low-resolution inputs while simultaneously preserving spatial detail and temporal coherence across frames. Existing methods suffer from a critical trade-off: GAN-based approaches produce perceptually sharp outputs but introduce temporal flickering, while recurrent propagation methods achieve smoothness at the cost of fine artistic texture.

This research proposes a hybrid deep learning framework that systematically combines BasicVSR's bidirectional recurrent propagation with Real-ESRGAN's GAN-based perceptual enhancement. Dense optical flow estimation via the Farneback algorithm provides motion-compensated frame warping, and a novel α-blending mechanism fuses the temporally-aligned output with the GAN-enhanced frame — offering a tuneable balance between sharpness and stability.

Experiments across five video categories (nature, sports, archival film, animation, and surveillance) demonstrate consistent improvement over state-of-the-art baselines in PSNR, SSIM, and temporal warp error. A user study with 85% preference confirms the perceptual superiority of the combined approach.

11 — Future Directions
Future Work
Next Step
Learned Optical Flow
Replace Farneback with end-to-end trained networks (RAFT, FlowFormer) for improved accuracy on complex motion and occlusion scenarios.
Architecture
Transformer Backbone
Incorporate Vision Transformers (Video Swin, TTVSR) for long-range temporal dependency modeling beyond the current recurrent approach.
Training
Adaptive α-Scheduling
Dynamically learn scene-adaptive α values based on local motion magnitude and texture complexity rather than a fixed global blending factor.
Deployment
Real-Time on Edge
Model distillation and TensorRT optimization for real-time streaming VSR on edge devices and mobile platforms at consumer scale.
